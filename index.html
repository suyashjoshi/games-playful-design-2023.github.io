About

Welcome to TIGHTROPE, a showcase of work by the 2022 MA Independent Games & Playable Experience Design cohort at Goldsmiths, University of London.

TIGHTROPE interprets tension in play, from curiosity and uncertainty to challenge and courage, across the spectrum of play between rules and make-believe.

Through puzzle games, interactive fiction, Kinect & VR experiences, and role play, we explore what it means to walk metaphorical tightropes - noticing, feeling, learning, trusting, wandering, being.

In this weekend of play, we invite you to walk with us, to leave the door open for the unknown, to see the value of tension in creating new conversations.

Artists
Aayush Shukla
Aayush (He/Him) is a game designer, programmer and a tech-artist. Having a deep interest in psychological horrors and atmospherics, he tends to specialize in horror games however, creates all sorts of playable experiences nontheless. His games and experiences feature a strong audiovisual thematic cohesion, thus improving the overall immersion for the players.
Alice Casey
Alice (she/her) is a game designer and educator with a keen interest in art that tells often-unheard stories. Her work focuses on her experiences with queer identity and disability, through short, vignette-like interactive pieces. Alice has previously worked as a coding and design instructor, helping people from diverse backgrounds express themselves through games and related artforms.
Brandon Davies
Brandon (He/Him) is focused on creating playable experiences that highlight narratives surrounding topics ranging from City Design, Life Choices and Memories. A game audio graduate and a learning technologist, he is also intent on combining these skills to create educational and experimental audio components for his games.
Chengzhi Lin
Daniel Cox
Daniel (Any) creates playable experiences of all kinds with a shared focus on playing at the boundaries of conventional game design. Their games often have an emphasis on narratives, personal creativity, and meaning-making all filtered through various genre or thematic tropes from magic and witchcraft to noir criminal investigation.
Georgie Freeman
Georgie (She/Her) is a writer whose bringing her exploration of stories into the world of video games. Her games tend to pivot around unique characters and exciting plots. She tends to explore the idea of interaction in narratives and giving the player autonomy in games.
Kristopher D'Alterio
Kristopher (He/Him) designs systems that allow for the experience of complex systems. He focuses on using these systems to explore different ways people think, create more meaningful educational experiences, and highlight the essential problem-solving models of a variety of contexts, both real and fantastical.
Lu Zhang
Louie (He/Him) is a game designer with a deep interest in all types of gameplays and presentation. His work focuses on the presentation, stylization and atmosphere of the game. He tends to explore the expression of reality through games, including traditions, cultures and civilizations.
Samantha Yuen
Sam (they/them) facilitates learning for change through the lens of experience design, serious games, creative computing, ontological coaching, and organizational development. Their practice is centered on awareness of the invisible - narratives, patterns, boundaries, resistances, emotions, differences, power, and beyond.
Shiyuan Yi
Yi (She/Her) is a person, maybe an illustrator.
Xingyi Wang
Cornelia (She/They) is a game / gamification / interaction designer. She likes to observe people's behavior in real life and on the Internet, and she also prefers to analyze the pain in her personal growth, which are used as inspirations to make a game experience that leads to thinking and criticism. She tends to experiment and combine across subjects and platforms, and is committed to creating games beyond the games.
Yifeng Yuan
Zhixuan Kang
Zimu Cheng
Zimu (He/Him) is a techno-artist interested in interactive experiences and immersive gameplay. He focuses on games beyond a mere source of joy and seeks the applied potential within games. His games utilize unconventional controls and rules and sometimes take the form of installation to create unique experiences.
Ziwei Liu
Ziwei (She/Her) is a designer with a strong obsession with worldview and logic. She fancies preparing a plausible story and complete logic chain for every game. Her work focuses more on storytelling and world-building, through which she can present those fantastic scenes in her mind as visible and interactable projects.
